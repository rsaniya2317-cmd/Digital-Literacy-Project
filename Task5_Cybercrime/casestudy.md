PART-A CASE STUDY
UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD
 
Nowadays, UPI or Online payment fraud has became one of the very common incidents in this digital world. It is one of the most common types of cybercrime where fraudsters trick users into sharing OTPs, PINs, or scanning QR codes to steal money. Some of the common types of UPI frauds are Phishing Scams, Fake UPI apps, SIM cloning, etc. In this case a college student received a phone call from a person pretending to be a bank officer. The caller said that there was a problem with the student's UPI account and it needed to be fixed immediately.

To solve this issue, the fraudster sent a payment request to the student's UPI app and told him to enter his UPI pin to receive money. The student unaware of this fraud and believing the caller's words entered his UPI pin without checking properly. Instead of receiving money, the amount was deducted from his bank account. After that, the fraudster tried to get more details like OTP and bank details, but the student realized something was wrong and stopped responding. 

Such scams usually target people who are unaware of how UPI works, elderly people, children, etc. The main consequences are stress, financial loss and misuse of personal data.

This case study shows that we should never trust unknown callers and must always remember that entering a UPI pin always means we are sending money, not receiving it. It is very important for everyone to be alert during online payments. Some of the effective strategies to prevent these kind of frauds are verify links and sender details, set transaction limits, regularly update your UPI app, etc.  

UPI frauds are a growing concern in India's digital payment landscape. By understanding the types of frauds, how they work, and the steps to prevent them, people can protect themselves from losing money.
